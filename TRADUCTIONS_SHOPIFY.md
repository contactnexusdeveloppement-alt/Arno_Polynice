## ETHICS_VALUE — Position 1 : Matières Responsables

### 🇬🇧 ENGLISH

**title** :
```
Responsible Materials
```

**text** :
```
At Arno Polynice, the question of materials is far more than a simple technical choice: it is a stance. Whenever possible, I favour natural fibres — cotton, linen, wool, viscose or silk — fibres that breathe, that age beautifully, and that maintain a healthier relationship with both the body and the environment. Natural materials have that rare ability to travel through time without losing their dignity, to acquire a patina rather than wear out, and it is precisely this longevity that mirrors the values the brand stands for. A garment well crafted in an honest material is already an act of resistance against fast fashion and its synthetic textiles designed for obsolescence.

That said, I want to remain transparent about a concrete reality: my sourcing depends in part on available stock, whether from the small resellers I work with or the end-of-roll fabrics I recover. I therefore sometimes work with materials that are not exclusively natural — not out of indifference, but out of pragmatism and honesty regarding my current situation. What never changes, however, is the rigour of the selection: whatever the material in my hands, it is systematically assessed for its quality, its finish, and its ability to stand with dignity within an Arno Polynice piece. As the brand grows, the goal is to move ever closer to a sourcing model based on natural, responsible materials — a gradual, deliberate, and sincere evolution.
```

Materiales Responsables

**text** :

En Arno Polynice, la cuestión del material es mucho más que una simple elección técnica: es una toma de posición. Siempre que es posible, privilegio los materiales naturales — el algodón, el lino, la lana, la viscosa o la seda — fibras que respiran, que envejecen bien y que mantienen una relación más sana con el cuerpo y con el entorno. Los materiales naturales tienen esa rara capacidad de atravesar el tiempo sin degradarse en la indignidad, de adquirir pátina en lugar de desgastarse, y es precisamente esa longevidad la que se corresponde con los valores que defiende la marca. Una prenda bien concebida en un material honesto es ya un acto de resistencia frente a la moda rápida y sus tejidos sintéticos pensados para la obsolescencia.

No obstante, quiero ser transparente sobre una realidad concreta: mi aprovisionamiento depende en parte de las existencias disponibles, ya sea a través de los pequeños revendedores con los que trabajo o de los fines de rollo que recupero. Por ello, a veces trabajo con materiales que no son exclusivamente naturales, no por desinterés, sino por pragmatismo y por honestidad respecto a mi situación actual. Lo que nunca cambia, en cambio, es la exigencia en la selección: sea cual sea el material que tengo entre las manos, se evalúa sistemáticamente por su calidad, su caída y su capacidad de integrarse con dignidad en una pieza Arno Polynice. El objetivo, a medida que la marca se desarrolla, es tender cada vez más hacia un aprovisionamiento de materiales naturales y responsables — una evolución progresiva, asumida y sincera.
```

## ETHICS_VALUE — Position 2 : Fabrication Artisanale

### 🇬🇧 ENGLISH

**title** :
```
Artisanal Craftsmanship
```

**text** :
```
     At Arno Polynice, every piece is a work in its own right, conceived and created with the care a garment built to last truly deserves. The making is resolutely artisanal, anchored in a piece-by-piece logic of creation. Each garment passes through my hands at every stage of its construction — from cutting to final assembly — which guarantees constant attention to detail, to finishing, and to the coherence of every realisation. This way of working is slow, demanding, but above all just: it ensures we never lose sight of what matters, namely the quality and the intention behind each creation.

     This personal approach is enriched by a precious collaboration with Marie Torres, an exceptional embroiderer who has learned and mastered Lunéville hand embroidery — an ancestral, refined and meticulous technique long associated with the greatest French haute couture houses. Calling on Marie means inscribing Arno Polynice within a living artisanal tradition, that of a savoir-faire transmitted with rigour and passion, which elevates each piece far beyond the simple garment.

     This approach is fully part of a vision of French manufacturing of which the brand is proud. Producing in France means choosing traceability, proximity, and the human dimension. It means working with artisans who have a name, a story, an expertise. It is also a commitment we fully embrace: doing things properly, here, with the hands of those who know. In a sector where "Made in France" is too often brandished as a mere selling point, Arno Polynice turns it into a concrete, daily reality — a promise kept, stitch after stitch.
```

## ETHICS_VALUE — Position 3 : Anti-gaspillage

### 🇬🇧 ENGLISH

**title** :
```
Zero-Waste Approach
```

**text** :
```
       The question of fabric lies at the heart of every project at Arno Polynice, and here too transparency is essential. At this stage of the brand's development, I source mainly from small local resellers, which allows me to present my projects, experiment with different materials, and work with a flexibility suited to my reality as an independent entrepreneur. I also turn to end-of-roll fabrics from the textile industry — those residual stocks that larger structures can no longer move — giving them a useful life where they might otherwise have ended up unused. 

      But beyond provenance, it is the selection that makes all the difference: each fabric is chosen with care, observed, touched, evaluated. I do not simply take what is available; I systematically seek the best quality accessible within my means, because even within constraints there is no question of compromising on what matters. This rigour in the choice of materials is, in my eyes, the first commitment I make to those who wear Arno Polynice pieces.
```

## ETHICS_VALUE — Position 4 : Transparence

**title** :
```
Transparency
```

**text** :
```
       At Arno Polynice, transparency is not a marketing argument: it is a founding value. Behind every piece there is one and the same person — myself, a 21-year-old student operating under micro-entrepreneur status, who designs, develops and imagines every creation with passion and conviction. There is no anonymous factory, no hidden intermediary: every creation is the fruit of artisanal and considered work. 

       I work in good conditions, at my own pace, striving to build something honest and lasting from the very beginning. As part of this approach, I have made the conscious choice to equip myself with second-hand tools — sewing mannequin, scissors, and other everyday equipment — not out of constraint, but out of conviction: giving a second life to gear that is still functional is already a first step toward more responsible fashion. 

      Arno Polynice is therefore a brand with a human face, free of pretension and empty rhetoric — just a young creator building his world stone by stone, with the means of the real and the ambition to do things right.

**excerpt** :
```
Let me tell you my story.
My name is Adelson Arno Polynice Paugain, I am 19 years old and I live in Épinal. Ever since I was a child, fashion has been part of who I am. I loved dressing up and putting outfits together — not to impress anyone, but simply because I couldn't help myself.

When I started a BTS Métiers de la Mode et du Vêtement at lycée Mendès-France, everything accelerated. I truly learned the craft — cutting, finishing, building a silhouette. And that is where I took the leap by officially launching my own line. To name it, I drew from who I am at my core: Arno, my French given name. Polynice, my Haitian name. Two words to tell the story of two cultures, but one single identity.

What I create is a wardrobe between chic and streetwear — fitted jackets, shirts, trousers in assertive colours. Unique pieces, conceived and crafted by hand. I draw inspiration from the street and from my everyday life.

     My signature touch? It is recognisable at first glance.
On every one of my pieces, I work patch pockets, layered and riveted at each corner. It is no trivial detail; it is my hallmark, the gesture that makes every creation immediately recognisable. 

     This choice comes from me, from my obsession with detail, with that little something that turns an ordinary garment into a unique piece. In luxury, it is often a nothing that makes everything. For me, that is what it is: rivets placed with precision, pockets built like architecture. Nothing is left to chance.

Today, I dream of making Arno Polynice a brand that truly counts.

A kid, a sewing machine, and a vision. 

What comes next? I am writing it now...
```
